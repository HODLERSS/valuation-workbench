Moat note

Method & Data Quality

Every company was fetched by an independent agent pulling stockanalysis.com (primary), finviz.com (independent second source), and Yahoo Finance (price), then a separate adversarial agent re-pulled finviz + Yahoo, recomputed the EV identity (market cap + net debt = EV) and multiple reconciliation (EV/EBITDA × EBITDA ≈ EV), and listed any field it could not confirm against a second source. Cross-source disagreements are flagged, never averaged.

Valuation history — new in this version, and how it was verified

The biggest gap before was the missing time dimension: every multiple was a point-in-time snapshot. This version adds, per company, the trailing valuation multiples for each of the last 5 fiscal years (P/E, P/S, P/FCF, EV/Sales, EV/EBITDA, EV/FCF), scraped deterministically (regex over the rendered table — not LLM number-reading) from stockanalysis.com's ratios page. That powers the per-company band charts (Companies tab), the vs-Own-5y heat-strip column on the Valuation table, and the sector vs-own-history medians.

  • Coverage: historical multiples on 65/68 names; renderable own-history bands on 63, with a percentile read on 62. The rest carry an explicit reason, never a misleading chart: 2 indices (tracked as index P/E in the banner above), SpaceX (private), and 2 names with no clean multiple history (a micro-cap miner + a just-IPO’d AI-chip name). One recent IPO (CRWV) shows its short path with a “range not meaningful yet” label. Names whose range is distorted by a near-zero-base year (e.g. MSTR, NBIS) are marked n/m rather than given a false cheap/rich call.
  • Verified two independent ways — not taken on faith: (1) the scraped current EV/EBITDA reconciles with the pre-existing snapshot for 67/67 names (0 drift >12%), re-confirming both numbers; (2) a historical cross-identity — for each past year, P/E×net-income and P/S×revenue must both equal that year’s market cap, so they must equal each other — holds on 146/179 year-points within 6%. Every exception is an already-flagged condition: an ADR reporting financials in home currency while ratios are USD-based (DTEGY, STNE), or a near-zero-earnings year where P/E is mechanically n.m. (CELH, NXT).
  • Honest limits: the free history is 5 years (not 10–20) and single-source (stockanalysis) — labeled as such, exactly like the existing 5-year financials. There is no free forward-multiple history, so the bands are trailing only (no faked “forward P/E ±1SD” band). Earnings-based multiples are suppressed for negative/volatile-EBITDA names (miners, neoclouds, cash-burners) and judged on EV/Sales + the cash trajectory instead — a P/E band on a loss-maker teaches the wrong lesson.

Quality criteria & self-scores

    The four payback metrics — and why they differ

    Each answers "how many years to earn back the price," but from a different seat and a different cash definition:

    MetricQuestionPairs price……with cash
    P/EYears to recoup my equity checkMarket cap (equity only)Net income (after interest & tax)
    EV/EBITDAYears to recoup the whole businessEV (equity + debt)EBITDA (pre-interest, pre-capex)
    EV/FCF…counting real cash after reinvestmentEVLevered FCF (OCF − capex)
    EV/uFCF…the truest, capital-structure-neutralEVUnlevered FCF (EBITDA − capex − cash taxes)

    The rule: the value metric must match the cash that belongs to the same people. Net income is shareholders-only → pair with market cap (P/E). EBITDA/FCF belong to the whole business → pair with EV. Mismatching them (e.g. EV/net-income) compares a whole-business price to shareholder-only cash — apples to oranges.

    Trailing vs forward P/E — always check which one. Throughout this product, “P/E (TTM)” is trailing: price ÷ the last 12 months of actual EPS. “Fwd P/E” is forward: price ÷ the next-12-month consensus estimate (usually non-GAAP). Forward sits below trailing when earnings are expected to grow — but mind the gap: for heavy stock-comp / acquisition-amortization names (e.g. CRM, trailing ≈19× vs forward ≈12×), much of the drop is a GAAP→non-GAAP basis switch, not pure growth. Compare like with like. The index figures in the regime banner and the Market tab are trailing (TTM); the company table shows both side by side.

    Why EV/uFCF is the honest one (and why I added it): EBITDA ignores the capex a business must spend to keep running. For asset-light software EBITDA ≈ cash; for a datacenter, GPU neocloud, or bitcoin miner, EBITDA wildly overstates cash. Two divergences in the table are the signal:

    • EV/uFCF >> EV/EBITDA → capex intensity. GOOGL's EV/EBITDA 27 balloons to EV/uFCF 254 on AI-datacenter capex; CCJ (uranium) 69→164. Asset-light names (STNE, GLOB, INMD) show almost no gap.
    • EV/uFCF > EV/FCF → stock-comp intensity. uFCF starts from EBITDA (which is after stock comp) and does not add it back, while levered FCF does. So for SBC-heavy tech the unlevered figure is more conservative — it treats dilution as the real cost it is.
    • neg = burns cash after capex. 16 names — every bitcoin miner and neocloud (MARA, RIOT, CLSK, CIFR, CRWV, IREN), plus AMZN and INTC (AI/fab capex) — generate positive EBITDA but negative unlevered FCF. EV/EBITDA makes them look investable; EV/uFCF tells the cash truth. This is the single most useful column for understanding your MARA / miner cohort.

    Sourcing & verification of this metric: EBITDA, capex, cash taxes (income-tax expense proxy), and EV are TTM from stockanalysis. Capex — the input that drives the metric — was cross-checked against SEC XBRL primary filings (MSFT 0.3%, AAPL 4.0% agreement); AMZN/GOOGL use non-standard XBRL tags so the automated SEC check was inconclusive, but their magnitudes (~$151B / ~$110B TTM) match public 2026 AI-capex guidance. The formula is the article's simplification: it omits working-capital change and uses tax expense for cash taxes — close enough for ranking, not a substitute for a full DCF. Caveat (a known simplification): because it starts from EBITDA rather than operating cash flow, EV/uFCF runs conservative for high-stock-comp names; read it alongside EV/FCF, not instead of it.

    Three decision aids added — and the honest limits on each

    Picked from a 7-idea slate scored by an independent 3-judge panel on user-value, data-accuracy, buildability, anti-bloat fit, and learning value. The three that cleared a high accuracy bar AND stay drill-down depth (not new clutter):

    • “To Median” — mechanical mean-reversion (column + card). The % move if a name’s headline multiple returned to its own 5-year median, holding today’s fundamentals: price × (median ÷ current). It is an illustrative anchor for “distance to its own normal,” not a price target — the 5y median itself can reflect past over/under-valuation (NVO shows +219% precisely because its pre-fear median was a premium). Shown only for the 47 names with a clean base; blanked for recent IPOs, distorted ranges, and n/m, never defaulted. Every shown figure recomputes to within $0.01.
    • Re-rating direction (card). A one-line directional read of whether the last year’s move was driven by the multiple or by earnings (INTC: the multiple; NVDA: earnings). Deliberately directional only, not an exact split — the multiple×fundamental identity does not cleanly reconcile (per-year figures are company totals, not per-share, and ADRs carry FX), so presenting a precise two-bar attribution would be false precision. That is also why the full decomposition idea was cut.
    • “vs sector” peer line (card). Each name’s multiple next to its sector’s median on the same metric, so it’s judged vs its own past and its peers. Labeled in-set sector median (n=…), not an industry benchmark; small sectors (n<4) are flagged indicative. The median is bound directly to the precomputed sector value (no client-side recompute), and the comparison is suppressed if the metric doesn’t match.
    • Ownership markers. Disabled in this public build (no holdings exposed).

    Tracking universe (Watch tier) — how the additions were chosen and verified

    Beyond the curated set, ~36 large-cap names were added as a tracking universe (Watch tier) to make the cross-sectional views representative — not a buy list (the Picks tab owns conviction). Selection was a stated rubric, not vibes:

    • Liquidity/size gate: US-listed or liquid ADR, >$10B market cap, real fundamentals.
    • Coverage-gap value: fills sectors the set was thin or absent in (financials, healthcare, staples, energy/industrials) so the Sectors heatmap and Value Map show genuine diversifiers — the point of de-risking the AI concentration.
    • AI value-chain completeness: the AI/semiconductor supply chain you track — foundry (TSM), litho/equipment (ASML, AMAT, LRCX), memory (MU), networking (ANET, MRVL), AI servers (SMCI, DELL), AI software (PLTR, CRWD, PANW, SNOW).
    • Peer/relative-value relevance: direct comparables to names you track or evaluate (MU ↔ SK Hynix, LLY ↔ NVO, UNH ↔ ELV) so the new “vs sector” medians mean something.

    Verification — same bar as the rest: each was fetched from stockanalysis (primary) and cross-checked against finviz + Yahoo by an independent adversarial agent that re-pulled price / market-cap / P–E, reconciled the EV identity, and flagged any >5% disagreement (never averaged). The 5-year multiple history was scraped deterministically and reconciled against the snapshot. Per-name confidence, disagreements, and unverified fields are carried in the data exactly as for the original set. Search (press /) and the Watch tier filter let you isolate or hide this universe on any tab.

    What is and is not fully verified

    • Verified to <2% across 2-3 sources: price (64/68 names), market cap, and the headline multiples.
    • Flagged disagreements (167 total): mostly forward-P/E (different consensus EPS) and EV/EBITDA (different add-back definitions) between stockanalysis and finviz — surfaced per company, primary value shown.
    • Single-sourced / "unverified" (411 field-notes): the per-year 5yr financials and the new 5yr valuation-multiple history (both from stockanalysis, sourced from filings, not independently re-keyed); EV/FCF and FCF-yield derivations; and DTEGY's full valuation (finviz was Cloudflare-blocked for it — price still confirmed via Yahoo).
    • Currency: all valuation/price in USD; ADR per-year financials (DTEGY EUR, ONON CHF, NVO DKK, RELX GBP) are in home currency — flagged on those cards. Do not compare their raw revenue line to USD names without FX.
    • Low-confidence rows: CSGP (earnings depressed by Homes.com spend), NXT & OSCR (turnarounds, thin/erratic margins), SpaceX (private estimate). EBITDA/FCF multiples for these are unreliable — use forward P/E + the trajectory instead.
    • TTM-vs-FY basis: some EV/EBITDA multiples are computed on trailing-twelve-month EBITDA while the trajectory table shows fiscal-year EBITDA; for fast-moving names (CELH, ONON) these differ — flagged on the cards.
